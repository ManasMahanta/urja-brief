## Market Pulse

- Benchmarks were roughly flat for the week as attention shifted to the primary
  market, where three mainboard IPOs and a clutch of SME issues jostled for
  attention.
- **Global cues** were mixed; US indices were steady, and the rupee was quiet.
- Secondary-market volumes thinned as some liquidity was parked in IPO
  applications.

## The Big Move

**What happened:** a heavily hyped consumer IPO listed with a modest pop, while a
less-talked-about industrials issue quietly listed higher. The grey-market
premium had implied nearly the opposite ranking.

**Why it matters:** the **grey market premium (GMP)** is an unofficial,
unregulated number that reflects short-term sentiment, not value — and it is
wrong often enough that treating it as a signal is a good way to get burned. The
listing-day move is driven by demand and sentiment on the day; the business
underneath is what decides the next three years.

## Results Radar

Results season is only just warming up, but an early-reporting mid-cap beat on
revenue and missed on margin — a useful reminder that "beat" and "miss" depend
entirely on _which_ line you look at.

## Sector Watch

New-age and consumer names drew the IPO spotlight, while defensives were quiet.
Rotation into freshly-listed stocks is common right after a big listing and often
fades — worth watching, not chasing.

## IPO & Listings

Before applying to any IPO, the sane checklist is on our [IPO Center](/ipo): read
the **DRHP**, check whether it's a fresh issue or an offer-for-sale, compare the
valuation to listed peers, and ignore the GMP. Oversubscription also means you
may not get an allotment at all.

## Lightning Round

- FIIs were mild net sellers in the secondary market during the IPO rush.
- A large-cap announced a bonus issue; remember, a bonus changes the share count,
  not the value of your holding.
- Gold firmed slightly on global risk sentiment.

## One Idea

Pick one recently-listed company and read the "objects of the issue" section of
its DRHP — where the money is actually going. It's the single most clarifying page
in any prospectus, and pure research practice, not a recommendation.
