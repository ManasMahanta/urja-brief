## Market Pulse

- **Nifty 50** and the **Sensex** both closed at fresh record highs, led by
  private banks and autos; market breadth, though, was narrower than the headline
  suggests.
- **Bank Nifty** outperformed as large private lenders extended their run on
  steady credit-growth commentary.
- **Nifty IT** was the drag of the week, slipping on soft near-term guidance from
  a couple of the majors.

## The Big Move

**What happened:** the index made a new high, but on fewer stocks than a healthy
breakout usually needs — a handful of heavyweight financials and auto names did
most of the lifting while the broader market was flat to lower.

**Why it matters:** narrow rallies can keep the index climbing while most
portfolios go nowhere. It's a reminder that "the market is at an all-time high"
and "my stocks are at all-time highs" are two very different statements. When you
read a record-high headline, it's worth checking the advance-decline breadth
underneath it rather than the number alone.

## Results Radar

- A large private bank reported steady loan growth with stable asset quality;
  the **net interest margin** commentary is the line the Street parsed hardest.
- An IT major trimmed its near-term revenue outlook. The stock fell not on the
  quarter itself but on the **guidance** — a recurring lesson that, for IT,
  the forward view usually moves the price more than the trailing number.

## Sector Watch

Money rotated visibly from IT toward financials and autos this week. Whether
that's a durable shift or a short-term rotation is exactly the kind of thing to
watch over several weeks rather than call after one.

## IPO & Listings

A quiet week on the primary market, with a couple of SME issues open. As always,
the DRHP — not the grey-market chatter — is where the real homework lives.

## Lightning Round

- Foreign investors were net buyers for the week; domestic funds also added.
- The rupee held in a tight range against the dollar.
- Crude ticked lower, a mild positive for oil-importing sectors.

## One Idea

If you only do one thing this week: pull up the **advance-decline** line
alongside the index chart. Learning to read breadth is one of the cheapest
upgrades to how you see the market — a way to _understand_ rallies, not a signal
to trade them.
